There’s a lake in Africa that literally turns animals into stone-looking statues.

Lake Natron in Tanzania is extremely salty and has a pH level between 9 and 10.5 — almost as high as ammonia. When birds and small animals fall into the lake, the minerals in the water calcify their bodies, coating them in a hard shell. They don’t actually turn to stone, but they become perfectly preserved, statue-like figures.

reason this happens:

  • he lake is fed by mineral-rich hot springs.
  • Because it’s in a very hot climate, most of the water evaporates.
  1. So the lake becomes more and more concentrated with sodium carbonate and other minerals.

Fun twist:The lake is deadly for most animals, but flamingos actually thrive there because their skin and legs can handle the extreme conditions.



A huge portion of the world’s flamingos hatch right next to a lake that would kill most creatures. So it’s basically: a terrifying death lake for most animals and a safe flamingo nursery at the same time. jump to top
