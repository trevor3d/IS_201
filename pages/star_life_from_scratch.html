The Life of a Star

These some of the most remarkable objects in the universe. Here is the birth, life, and death of stars.


Birth

The interstellar medium is the matter and energy that exist in the space between stars within a galaxy. A group of matter is called a nebula

All it takes is enough gravity for the material to start collpasing in on itself and a burst of energy from an outside source to being star formation.

Spinning?

  • If the material around the center of mass begins to rotate the material around it, two things can happen:
    1. Split into multiple clouds to make either a cluster of stars or a binary system
    2. Form a disk to then potentially form a planetary solar system.

Gravity vs. pressure

  • pressure from the heated center starts pushing back against the collapsing force of gravity

Fusion of hydron into helium

  • You can't have a star without fusion
  • The core must heat up to at least 18 million degrees Fahrenheit!

Main Sequence (adult life)

As long as theres enough hydrogen fuel, a star can live for trillions of years

The different types of stars:

By temperature and color:

    O – Blue, extremely hot, very massive
    B – Blue-white, very hot
    A – White
    F – Yellow-white
    G – Yellow (the Sun is G-type)
    K – Orange
    M – Red, coolest and most common

Variable stars: a star whose brightness changes over time as seen from Earth.

Binary Stars: One star orbiting another

  • They cover 50% of the stars in the sky

Death

What happens when the hydrogen runs out in the core?

  1. There's still hydrogen on the outside
  2. The core shrinks in size releases more heat, creating more energy
  3. The outside of the star gets larger overall and becomes a red giant

When the hydrogen is gone...

Stars smaller than 4 solar masses:

  1. A series of termal pulses will start, shedding off layers of the atmosphere
  2. what's left behind? A plnetary neubula with a white dwarf at it's center

Stars larger than 4 solar masses (much bigger than the sun):

  1. Fuse heavier and heavier elements (up to iron)
  2. Core collapses suddenly
  3. Outer layers explode in a supernova
    What's left behind? A Neutron star or a black hole(if it was extremely massive)

The phrase, "we are made from star dust" is true because supernovas leave behind all sorts of material.
